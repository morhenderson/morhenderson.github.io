Portfolio

All of the programs discussed on this page can be found on my personal GitHub, either in their own repos or the Code-Samples repo which contains a few simple example programs I've written.

Filtering Algorithms

I first learned about Kalman filters in a graduate level course titled "Statistical Estimation for Dynamical Systems". Later I served as teaching assistant for the course and took an advanced section that dove deeper into the theory and included Bayesian, particle, and other filter methods.

Buffmo

As TA for the Statistical Estimation course, I decided to write a basic but thorough tutorial on the standard Kalman Filter (including truth modeling) applied to a simple linear dynamical system. It includes a Python program (Buffmo.py) and a comprehensive writeup (Buffmo.pdf) covering the mathematical formulation of the problem and detailed descriptions of the program.


Download Buffmo.py |  Download Buffmo.pdf

OD-Filters

After my semester as TA, I was impressed by the final projects students had submitted and decided to revisit my own project from the course with the benefit of a few years of practice. The theme of the project is statistical orbit determination for non-linear orbital dynamics, and includes symplectic Verlet integration for truth modeling as well as linearized, extended, and unscented Kalman filters (LKF, EKF, & UKF).


Download OD-Filters.zip |  Download OD-Writeup.pdf

Machine Learning

I became interested in machine learning as an undergraduate, when I began to experiment with simple methods like genetic algorithms. During my summer with the NASA Frontier Development Lab, I gained some education and hands-on experience with neural networks, and now have some modest experience applying ML in research settings.

Myc-Drop

I wrote the Myc-Drop program specifically for this portfolio. It creates and trains a simple fully-connected feed-forward network, with architecture and learning parameters set by the user, to classify samples from a classic mycological data set as either poisonous ("drop") or safe ("keep").
The companion program, "Myc-Check", compares the performance of various choices of architecture and learning rates, and employs a user-defined weighting scheme for performance metrics.


Download Myc-Drop.zip |  Download Myc-Drop.pdf

Nevermore

The Nevermore program is a Poe-etic variation of the classic genetic algorithm problem in which fitness among a population of strings is determined by the number of characters shared with a target string. The premise is extremely simple, but I feel it provides an intuitive demonstration of the GA concept.


Download Nevermore.zip |  Download Nevermore.txt

Lattice Dynamics

The primary focus of my research as a graduate student was thermal transport in nanoengineerd semiconductor matrials. One of the central themes in this research was the study of phonons through lattice dynamics models which explore the atomistic vibrational properties of materials in a manner similar to the analysis of normal modes in the field of classical mechanics.
